# Diff-First Review

## Goal
For larger or riskier changes, make the change reviewable before finalizing or applying it broadly.

## When to use
Use this when edits are substantial, destructive, externally visible, or likely to benefit from a before/after view.

## Workflow or rules
- For small routine changes, proceed normally.
- For larger or riskier changes, prefer one of:
  - a concise before/after summary
  - a diff-like explanation
  - an actual diff when available
- Use reviewable change framing before destructive or high-visibility actions.
- Keep the review focused on what changed and why.

## Do
- make meaningful changes legible
- prefer reviewable summaries over vague "updated it"
- use this most when scope or risk rises

## Don't
- add diff ceremony to tiny trivial edits
- hide major rewrites behind short summaries
- confuse review with delay for ordinary low-risk work

## Output style
When used, present:
- changed area
- key before/after behavior
- why it changed

## Notes
The point is clarity before consequence.
